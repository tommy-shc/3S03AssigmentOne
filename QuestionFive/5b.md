After generating the tests it took about ~45 minutes to reach 90% coverage. The strategy that I followed involved initially generating a test suite, followed by manual debugging, and some final edge case refinement with a few repeated iterations. The role of the AI in this strategy was to do the bulk of the coding and commenting. Most of the time, the AI would generate enough code to meet a large percentage in coverage. Afterwards, as the human I would need to fix up most of the code to work with the local workspace and compilation errors. With a working testing suite, I would prompt to AI to generate more tests to reach closer to the desired 90% code coverage. Thus as the human, I played the role of a quality/assurance check, making sure the code worked and made sense in the context of our project.
